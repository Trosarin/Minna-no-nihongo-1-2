formusayExample

Particle overview (は / が / を / に / で / へ)

ParticleMain functionExample
はtopic (known). contrast. Never in the relative clause.私わたしは 学生がくせいです。
がSubject (new/unexpected). Answer on question word. For 好すき/わかる/ある/Potential. In the relative clause.猫ねこが います。
をDirect Object. For motion verbs: Strecke (公園こうえんを 歩あるく).本ほんを 読よむ。
にgoal/direction. time point. location of existence (います). Indirect object. recipient.学校がっこうに 行いく。 7時しちじに 起おきる。
でplace to action. mys/tool. material. reason (Adj/N).図書館としょかんで 勉強べんきょうする。 バスで 行いく。

⚠️ Common pitfalls

に vs で: 公園こうえんに いる (location of existence) ↔ 公園こうえんで 走はしる (place of action).

は vs が: topic (known) vs. Subject (new/answer). In the relative clause only が.

を vs が at Potential: 漢字かんじが 読よめる (not を!).

Understanding conjugation logic

① Recognizing the verb group

group II (一段いちだん): Ends with -iる or -eる. Conjugates simply: る removed, now ending added.

group I (五段ごだん): All other verbs (-う/-く/-ぐ/-す/-つ/-ぬ/-ぶ/-む). vowel before る switches.

Irregular: Only 2 verbs — する and 来くる.

⚠️ Fase friends — these look like Gr. II (-iru/-eru), but they are Group I:

帰かえるto return home
入はいるenter
走はしるto run
知しるto know
切きるto cut
要いるneed (different from いる 'to be'!)
減へるdecrease

② The stem system for Group II

For Gr. II the logic is simple: る remove → the this is stem. All endings are directly attached:

stem食たべ-
+ ます食たべます
+ て食たべて
+ た食たべた
+ ない食たべない
+ られる (Pot/Pass)食たべられる
+ させる (causative)食たべさせる
+ よう (Volitional)食たべよう

💡 For Gr. II aso: one stem, everything is attached. Very beginner-friendly.

③ The vowel change in Group I

For Gr. I the vowel changes before る/く/む/etc. depending on form. For example 書かく (kaku):

formvowelExample

💡 Memory-Mnomonic: a-stem = nogative/passive world. i-stem = polite. e-stem = can/when/command. o-stem = will.

④ Special case: う-verbs

verbs on -う become before a-endings a わ (no あ!):

⑥ Confusion pairs — when to use what?

Here are the most frequent stumbling blocks of L26-50 as pairs next each other. Each box handles ONE pair — when you the distinction is difficult, this is your card.

🔀 ように vs. ために — both my 'in order to'

Key question: Can you directly control the goal?

ためにL42 · active, controllableI do something actively to achieve Y. Y is a intentional action (buy, meet, save).家いえを 買かうために、貯金ちょきんする。In order to buy a house, I am saving.
ようにL36 · non-deliberateThe goal is ability/understanding/avoidance — something, twon't directly controllable by will is.忘わすれないように、メモする。So I don't forget it, I make a note.

💡 Quick test: Does the goal part start with a ability verb (話はなせる, 見みえる, 分わかる) or a negation (〜ない)? → ように. Otherwise → ために.

🔀 そうです — three different myings!

Key question: where does the information come from?

Adj-そうL40 · APPEARANCE for adjectiveい drops away + そう: おいしそうですLooks delicious (judging by appearance)
Vます-stem-そうL43 · APPEARANCE for verb降ふりそうですLooks like rain (visual impression)
Plain + そうだL47 · HEARSAY降ふるそうだ / 〜に よると 〜そうだThey say, it rains (heard, read)

💡 Mnomonic: See → form changes (い gone / ます-stem). Hearing → plain form remains unchanged + そうだ.

🔀 ている vs. てある vs. ておく — state vs. Preparation

Key question: Am I describing a state or an action? Who has something before?

N が V自じ-ているL29 · neutral stateIntransitive verb + ている = thing is in a state窓まどが 開あいて いる。The window is open (regardless why).
N が V他た-てあるL30 · intentionally preparedTransitive verb + てある = someone has set it up intentionally, state remains窓まどが 開あけて ある。The window was (by someono) intentionally openod.
N を V-ておくL30 · I do it now in advanceActive action in the do in advance窓まどを 開あけて おく。I do the window (new) on (for later).

⚠️ Particle case: てある requires が (the Object is at the Subject!), not を. ておく uses normal を. ている with intransitive verbs takes が.

🔀 の vs. こと — Nominalization

Key question: Are you talking about a concrete experience or about a abstract fact?

V-Plain + のL38 · concrete, experienceableFor sensory experience, see/hear, Preferences. mandatory for 好すき/嫌きらい/上手じょうず/下手へた/見みる/聞きく.歌うたうのが 好すきです。Singenoral likes I.
V-Plain + ことL18/L38 · abstract, objectiveFor abstractn statements, rules, facts. mandatory for ことができる / ことがある / ことにする.運動うんどうすることは 大切たいせつです。Sport to treiben is important.

💡 Rule of thumb: For sensations/sensory perception → の. For abilities/rules/decisions → こと. ⚠️ NOT with もの confuse — もの denotes physical things or has special myings (ものです 'is just like it is'); is not a normal Nominalization.

🔀 もらう / くれる / あげる — the three giving verbs

Key question: Who is Subject (= who does the sentence)? In which direction goes the gift?

もらうL24 / pol.: いただく L41I am the Subject, hole/receive from someone giver with に or から.私わたしは 友達ともだちに 本ほんを もらった。I became by the friend a book.
くれるL24 / pol.: くださる L41JEMAND is Subject, gives ME. giver with が, I am recipient.友達ともだちが 私わたしに 本ほんを くれた。Der friend gave me a book.
あげるL24 / pol.: さしあげる L41ICH is Subject, give to someone recipient with に.私わたしは 友達ともだちに 本ほんを あげた。I gave the friend a book.

💡 Arrow mnomonic: くれる comes TO me (arrow rein). あげる goes AWAY from me (arrow raus). もらう I get (I reach). ⚠️ In Japanese ALWAYS from own perspective: くれる only when I (or my group) the recipient is.

🔀 られる — the TRIPLE identity (most important confusion in intermediate material!)

Key question: Which function has られる here? There are three.

① 受身うけみ (passive)L37 · 'is 'Subject is AFFECTED. actor often with に. Context: personal action, with harm/benofit.先生せんせいに 褒ほめられた。I was praised by the teacher.
② 可能かのう (Potential)L27 · 'can'Subject CAN something. Object with が (not を!).刺身さしみが 食たべられる。I can Sashimi to eat.
③ 尊敬語そんけいご (respect)L49 · higher-ranking Personverb form for the action of a superior/customers. form identical to passive.社長しゃちょうが 来こられた。The company president has come. (pol.)

⚠️ Only at group II (食たべる→食たべられる) and irreg. (来くる→来こられる) all three forms are IDENTICAL. For group I sees it so by: 書かく → Potential 書かける ≠ passive 書かかれる. 💡 How distinguish? Subject + Particle andchauen: 私わたしが…られる → passive. 私わたしは…が食たべられる → Potential. 先生せんせいが…られる + more polite Context → respect.

🔀 のに (concession) vs. のに (purpose) — same form, completely different mying

Key question: What comes after のに?

〜のに …L45 · althoughconcession. After のに comes something surprising/disappointing. plain form (for な-adj/N: な+のに).勉強べんきょうしたのに、失敗しっぱいした。Obwherehl I learned have, at the I durchgefall.
〜のに + 使つかう/いいL42 · in order to / forpurpose/suitability. After のに comes 使つかう / いい / 便利べんり / 必要ひつよう / 時間じかんがかかる. Only 辞書形じしょけい before.これは 野菜やさいを 切きるのに 使つかう。Ono uses this, in order to cut vegetables.

💡 Quick test: Does のに follow a 使つかう/いい/便利べんり/必要ひつよう? → purpose のに (L42). Stands a unexpected consequence? → concessioniv-のに (L45).
